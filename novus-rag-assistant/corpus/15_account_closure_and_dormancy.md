# Account Closure and Dormancy Policy — Novus Bank

## Account Dormancy

### Definition
An account is classified as **dormant** if there are no customer-initiated transactions (debit or credit) for **24 consecutive months**.

- **Customer-initiated transactions** include: withdrawals, fund transfers, bill payments, deposits, cheque clearance.
- **System-generated debits** (maintenance fees, interest credits) do NOT reset the dormancy clock.
- Interest continues to be credited to dormant accounts.

### Dormancy Stages
| Period of Inactivity | Status | Restrictions |
|---------------------|--------|-------------|
| 12 months | **Inactive** | Online banking login generates warning; no restrictions |
| 24 months | **Dormant** | All debit transactions blocked; credits still allowed |
| 10 years | **Unclaimed Deposit** | Transferred to RBI's Depositor Education and Awareness (DEA) Fund |

### Notifications Before Dormancy
- Novus Bank sends reminders at:
  - 18 months: Email + SMS alert — "Your account will become dormant in 6 months if no transaction is made."
  - 23 months: App notification + email — "Your account becomes dormant next month."
- Customers should make at least one transaction to reset the inactivity timer.

### Reactivating a Dormant Account
1. Visit any Novus branch (in-person visit mandatory — online reactivation not available for dormant accounts).
2. Submit: Reactivation request form + KYC documents (Aadhaar + PAN) + a transaction (minimum ₹1,000 deposit or withdrawal).
3. **Reactivation fee:** ₹500 (Standard), ₹250 (Plus), Free (Elite).
4. Processing time: Same day if branch visit before 3:00 PM; next working day otherwise.
5. After reactivation: Account fully functional within 2 hours of processing.

### DEA Fund (Unclaimed Deposits)
- Accounts inactive for **10 years** with unclaimed balances are transferred to the RBI DEA Fund.
- Transfer happens on January 1 each year for accounts that crossed 10 years during the previous calendar year.
- **Reclaiming from DEA Fund:** Customer can file a claim with Novus Bank → Novus Bank claims from RBI on customer's behalf.
- Reclaim process: 30–45 working days after submission of proof of ownership.
- No loss of principal; interest accrued up to transfer date is also recoverable.

## Account Closure

### Voluntary Closure (Customer-Initiated)

**Timing restriction:**
- Accounts closed within **6 months** of opening: Early closure fee of ₹500 (waived for Plus/Elite).
- After 6 months: No closure fee.

**Pre-conditions for closure:**
1. Zero outstanding balance in all linked loan accounts.
2. No active fixed deposits (must be prematurely broken or transferred first).
3. No pending disputes or fraud investigations.
4. All standing instructions and autopay mandates cancelled by customer.
5. Linked SIPs (mutual funds) must be stopped via investment section before closure.
6. Debit card returned or customer declaration that it has been destroyed.
7. Minimum balance (if any) cleared.

**Closure process:**
1. App: Profile → Account Settings → Close Account (available only if all pre-conditions met).
2. Or in-branch with written request and account passbook/card surrender.
3. Final balance paid via: cheque (account name) or NEFT to another bank account.
4. Timeline: Processing in **5 working days** of request submission.
5. Confirmation: Email sent when closure is complete.

### Involuntary Closure (Bank-Initiated)
Novus Bank may close an account under:
- Regulatory/court order.
- Persistent KYC non-compliance (>6 months after due date).
- Suspicious activity confirmed by compliance team.
- Account being used for fraudulent purposes.

**Notice for involuntary closure:**
- 30-day written notice sent to registered email and mobile (except for regulatory orders, which may be immediate).
- Customer can appeal within 15 days by contacting L2 Grievance team.

## Savings Account — Minimum Balance Rules on Closure
- If closing with balance below minimum balance limit: MAB charge is NOT applied for the closure month.
- Only actual balance is paid out.

## Joint Account Closure
- For joint accounts: **Both/all holders** must consent to closure (written request from all account holders required).
- "Either or Survivor" operating mode: Any one survivor can close account if other holder is deceased (death certificate + succession documents required).

## Minor Account Closure or Conversion
- Minor accounts (held in guardian's name): Convert to regular account when minor turns 18 (mandatory within 6 months of 18th birthday; account restricted if not converted).
- Conversion requires: New KYC in minor's own name + minor's PAN + fresh account opening form.
- Alternatively, close the minor account and open a fresh adult account.

## Nomination on Closure/Death
- If account holder deceased: Nominee receives balance after submission of death certificate + nominee's KYC + claim form.
- If no nominee: Legal heir certificate required (process takes 45–90 days depending on documentation).
- Outstanding loan balances of deceased are settled from account balance before payout to nominee.

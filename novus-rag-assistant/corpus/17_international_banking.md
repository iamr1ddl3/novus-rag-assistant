# International Banking — Novus Bank

## Liberalised Remittance Scheme (LRS)

### Overview
Under RBI's LRS, resident Indians can remit up to **USD 2,50,000 per financial year** (approximately ₹2.07 crore at current rates) without RBI approval, for permitted purposes.

### Permitted Purposes
- Education abroad (tuition fees, living expenses).
- Medical treatment abroad.
- International travel (foreign exchange for personal use).
- Investment in foreign securities (shares, bonds, real estate).
- Gifts and donations to relatives abroad.
- Maintenance of close relatives abroad.

### Not Permitted Under LRS
- Purchase of lottery tickets, banned publications.
- Remittance to countries under FATF non-compliant list (currently: Iran, North Korea, Myanmar).
- Capital account transactions not explicitly permitted.

### LRS Through Novus Bank
- Available via Novus app → International → Send Money Abroad.
- Or via branch (required for amounts above ₹25 lakh equivalent).
- **TCS (Tax Collected at Source):** 20% TCS on LRS remittances above ₹7 lakh per financial year (per Budget 2023). TCS is creditable against income tax liability.
- Documents required: Purpose declaration, invoice/admission letter (for education), or self-declaration (for travel/gifts).

## SWIFT Transfers (Outward)

### Process
- Initiate via app (up to ₹10 lakh equivalent) or branch (above ₹10 lakh).
- Provide: Beneficiary name, IBAN/Account number, SWIFT/BIC code, Bank name and address, Purpose of remittance.

### Timelines
| Transfer Amount | Typical Settlement |
|----------------|--------------------|
| Up to ₹5 lakh | 2–3 working days |
| ₹5 lakh – ₹25 lakh | 3–4 working days |
| Above ₹25 lakh | 4–5 working days |

Settlement times depend on correspondent bank processing and beneficiary bank location.

### Charges (Outward SWIFT)
| Amount | Charge |
|--------|--------|
| Up to ₹1 lakh equivalent | ₹500 |
| ₹1 lakh – ₹5 lakh equivalent | ₹750 |
| ₹5 lakh – ₹25 lakh equivalent | ₹1,000 |
| Above ₹25 lakh equivalent | ₹1,500 |

- Plus customers: 20% discount on SWIFT charges.
- Elite customers: SWIFT charges waived (up to 4 transfers/month).
- Correspondent bank charges (NOSTRO deduction): ₹300–₹800 depending on destination country (deducted at beneficiary end unless "OUR" option chosen; "OUR" adds ₹500 extra).

### Inward Remittances
- Foreign inward remittances credited in INR at RBI reference rate + bank markup (0.5% for Novus).
- Credits to savings account within 1 working day of funds reaching Novus's NOSTRO account.
- Purpose of inward remittance declaration (FIRA — Foreign Inward Remittance Advice) issued automatically; downloadable from app.

## NRI Banking

### Account Types for NRIs

**NRE (Non-Resident External) Account:**
- Holds INR equivalent of foreign earnings.
- **Fully repatriable** — principal and interest can be transferred abroad without restriction.
- Interest is tax-free in India.
- Joint holding: with another NRI or with a resident Indian on "Former or Survivor" basis only.
- Acceptable for: maintaining Indian investments, paying Indian bills.

**NRO (Non-Resident Ordinary) Account:**
- Holds income earned in India (rent, dividends, pension).
- **Repatriation limit:** Up to USD 1 million per financial year (net of taxes) with CA certificate.
- Interest taxable in India at 30% TDS (lower rate if DTAA applies).
- Joint holding: with resident Indian permitted.

**FCNR(B) (Foreign Currency Non-Resident Bank) Account:**
- Term deposit only (1–5 years).
- Held in foreign currency (USD, EUR, GBP, JPY, AUD, CAD, SGD).
- Fully repatriable; interest and principal tax-free in India.
- Exchange risk borne by Novus Bank — no currency conversion loss for customer at maturity.
- Interest rates set quarterly; check novusbank.in for current rates.

### NRI Account Opening
- In person at any Novus branch (while on India visit), OR
- Courier-based: Download NRI account opening form, get notarized abroad, courier to Novus Bank Bengaluru processing center.
- Video KYC available for NRI savings accounts.
- Documents: Valid passport, valid visa/OCI/PIO card, overseas address proof, photograph, PAN (or Form 60 if PAN not held).

## International Debit Card Usage

- By default, international usage is disabled on all Novus debit cards.
- Enable via: Novus app → Card → International Transactions → Enable (choose countries/regions).
- Daily withdrawal limit (international ATMs): ₹25,000 equivalent.
- International POS (point of sale): up to ₹1,00,000/day.
- Foreign currency conversion markup: **3.5%** for Standard; **2.5%** for Plus; **1.5%** for Elite.
- Disable immediately after travel via app to prevent misuse.

## Multi-Currency Forex Card
- Separate prepaid card; not linked to savings account.
- Load up to 12 currencies simultaneously.
- No markup on loaded currencies (spend-as-loaded).
- Reload via app; unused balance refunded to savings account within 3 working days of surrender.
- ATM cash withdrawals abroad: ₹150 per transaction.
- Emergency replacement abroad: Available via Visa/Mastercard emergency assistance (charges apply).
